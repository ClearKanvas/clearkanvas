# ClearKanvas Website: Structure & Copy

*Where complexity becomes clarity.*

This document gives you the full website blueprint: how many top-level options, what sits in each, the Services mega menu, every supporting page, and the actual copy to use. It is informed by how leading GBS firms (Auxis, WNS, Genpact) structure their sites, adapted to your stage and positioning as senior operators serving the US, UK, and GCC from Pakistan.

## Part 1: Structure at a glance

### Top-level navigation: 5 items

A lean header is a premium signal. Five is ideal here. We are launching client-facing only (no blog or careers yet), so the header stays focused on selling. The contact action is a button, not a plain link, pinned to the top right in your accent color (this is the convention on the majority of B2B sites).

| # | Nav item | Type | Purpose |
|---|----------|------|---------|
| 1 | **Services** | Mega menu (hover) | What we do, grouped into themes |
| 2 | **Industries** | Dropdown | Who we serve, by sector |
| 3 | **How We Work** | Dropdown | Engagement models, process, why us, why Pakistan |
| 4 | **About** | Dropdown | Story, mission, values, leadership, locations |
| 5 | **Contact** | Button (accent) | Book a call, reach us |

*Held for later: Insights (articles, guides, case studies) and Careers. Both are easy to add when you are ready. For now the site is a client-facing presence only.*

A note on labels: SEO and clarity experts warn that generic words like "Services" and "Solutions" do not describe what you do. Two safe options: keep "Services" (familiar, expected on GBS sites) but make every sub-item descriptive, or rename it "What We Do." I recommend keeping **Services** for familiarity and loading the descriptions into the mega menu.

## Part 4: Things you did not ask for, that premium GBS sites include

I have folded these into the structure above; calling them out so you can decide what to keep:

1. **Engagement Models** as its own concept. Buyers want to know *how* they can work with you, not just *what* you do. This is a strong differentiator and reduces friction.
2. **A "Why Pakistan" page.** Your delivery base is an advantage, not something to hide. Auxis leads with its hubs (Costa Rica, Colombia); you should own Pakistan with the same confidence.
3. **Build-Operate-Transfer** called out explicitly. It is a premium, high-trust offering that signals you think long term about the client, not just the contract.
4. **Insights / Resources.** Even one or two genuinely useful articles or a downloadable guide builds authority and feeds SEO. Phase 2 is fine.
5. **A persistent "Book a discovery call" CTA** in the header and repeated through the page. Low-friction, no hard sell.
6. **Service-level agreements and outcomes language** ("agreed service levels," "measured by outcomes"). This is how serious buyers separate operators from freelancers.
7. **Trust and security signals.** A short line on data handling, confidentiality, and (later) any certifications. Enterprise buyers look for this.
